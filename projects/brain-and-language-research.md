# Brain × Language Models — Cognitive-Neuroscience Research

**Role:** researcher (first author) · **Output:** 3 peer-reviewed papers, 3 best-paper awards · **Where:** SNU (Brain & Cognitive Science), Korea University (Brain & Cognitive Engineering / BSPL)

The research foundation behind the applied LLM work: studying how **human brains** and
**artificial language models** represent and process meaning. This is *why* the production
work cares so much about grounding, faithfulness, and honest evaluation — it comes from a
cognitive-science view of what "understanding" actually is.

## Publications

### Metaphor in Mind and Machine — OHBM 2024
*Hyeonseop Yoon, Shin-ae Yoon (Organization for Human Brain Mapping).*
Published in **Aperture Neuro** (OHBM 2024 Annual Meeting). [doi:10.52294/001c.120592](https://doi.org/10.52294/001c.120592)
🏆 **Best Paper award (poster) — Max Planck Institute for Human Cognitive & Brain Sciences.**

A study of **metaphor processing across brain and machine** — comparing how the human brain
and language models handle figurative/non-literal meaning. Presented at the 30th OHBM Annual
Meeting (Seoul, 2024) and recognized with a Max Planck best-paper award.

### Comparative Analysis of Brain and NLP Models for Reasoning Tasks — BESK 2023
*Hyeonseop Yoon, Juhyeon Lee, Jong-Hwan Lee.*
Brain Engineering Society of Korea. 🏆 **Best Paper (poster), NeuroImage & AI.**

Aligns **neural representations with NLP-model representations on reasoning tasks**, probing
where brains and models agree and diverge.

### Korean Twitter Bot Detection based on Deep Learning — KSC 2022
*Hyeonseop Yoon, Jeong-Sik Park.*
Korea Software Congress (KIISE). 🏆 **Best Paper (Language Technology).**

A deep-learning system for **detecting automated/bot accounts in Korean social-media text** —
an early applied-NLP project on Korean-language modeling and classification.

## Through-line to the applied work
- **Grounding & faithfulness** in the [contact-center QA paper](grounded-qa-contact-center.md) is the engineering expression of a neuroscience question: what does it mean for a system to *mean* what it says?
- **Brain–NLP alignment** → representation/embedding work (the [retrieval embeddings](korean-retrieval-embeddings.md)).
- **Signal processing on neural data** → the [rtFIN-NL neurofeedback](neurofeedback-rtfin.md) collaboration and the rPPG sensing work.
